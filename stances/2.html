State's Rights mentions how much power the states get in comparison to the central government.

As a Democratic-Republican, we believe in keeping most of our power in the states. The Democratic-Republicans believe in a weak central government, because we remember the tough years before Independence, when we were ruled by Great Britain. When Great Britain ruled us, they had a strong central government, and that caused us US colonies to have little to no power. We don't want to recreate that, so we believe in keeping our power in the states, while our central government supports them.
